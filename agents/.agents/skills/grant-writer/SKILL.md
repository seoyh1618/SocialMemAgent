# Grant Writer

Create persuasive grant proposals that secure funding for nonprofits, research projects, and community initiatives. This skill helps you craft compelling narratives, demonstrate impact, and meet funder requirements to maximize your chances of approval.

Whether you're seeking foundation grants, government funding, corporate sponsorships, or research awards, this skill ensures your proposals are clear, credible, and aligned with funder priorities. It balances emotional storytelling with data-driven evidence to build a convincing case for support.

Ideal for nonprofit professionals, researchers, development officers, program managers, and organizations seeking funding to advance their mission and expand their impact.

## Core Workflows

### Workflow 1: Full Grant Proposal
1. **Funder Research** - Analyze priorities, past awards, submission requirements
2. **Executive Summary** - Write compelling 1-page overview of request
3. **Needs Statement** - Document problem with data and human stories
4. **Goals & Objectives** - Define SMART outcomes and measurable targets
5. **Methodology** - Detail how program will achieve stated goals
6. **Evaluation Plan** - Explain how success will be measured and reported
7. **Budget Narrative** - Justify expenses with clear explanations
8. **Organizational Capacity** - Demonstrate ability to execute
9. **Sustainability Plan** - Show long-term viability beyond grant period
10. **Supporting Documents** - Compile letters, financials, board info

### Workflow 2: Letter of Inquiry (LOI)
1. **Hook Opening** - Capture attention with compelling problem statement
2. **Organization Introduction** - Establish credibility in 2-3 sentences
3. **Project Overview** - Summarize what funding will accomplish
4. **Funding Request** - State specific amount and time period
5. **Unique Value** - Explain why you're best positioned to solve this
6. **Next Steps** - Invite full proposal or meeting
7. **Closing Impact** - End with vision of change you'll create

### Workflow 3: Budget Development
1. **Personnel Costs** - Salaries, benefits, consultants with justification
2. **Program Expenses** - Supplies, materials, services, travel
3. **Indirect Costs** - Administrative overhead, facilities, utilities
4. **Matching Funds** - Document committed resources from other sources
5. **Budget Narrative** - Explain rationale for each line item
6. **Cost Effectiveness** - Show efficient use of resources
7. **Multi-Year Planning** - Project future sustainability

### Workflow 4: Impact Reporting
1. **Outcomes Measurement** - Quantify results against stated goals
2. **Story Collection** - Gather compelling beneficiary testimonials
3. **Data Visualization** - Create charts, graphs showing progress
4. **Challenges & Adaptations** - Honest assessment of obstacles faced
5. **Lessons Learned** - Share insights for continuous improvement
6. **Future Plans** - Connect results to next phase of work
7. **Stewardship** - Thank funder, acknowledge partnership

## Best Practices

- **Align with funder** - Mirror their language, priorities, and values
- **Lead with impact** - Start with change you'll create, not what you need
- **Use data strategically** - Balance statistics with human stories
- **Write SMART goals** - Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound
- **Show track record** - Demonstrate past success and organizational capacity
- **Be realistic** - Don't overpromise results you can't deliver
- **Quantify everything** - "Serve 500 families" beats "help the community"
- **Address sustainability** - Show plan beyond grant funding
- **Follow instructions** - Meet every requirement, respect page limits
- **Proofread obsessively** - Errors signal lack of attention to detail
- **Get external review** - Fresh eyes catch gaps in logic
- **Build relationships** - Contact program officer before submitting
- **Tell stories** - Individual narratives make abstract problems real
- **Show innovation** - Explain what's unique about your approach
- **Document methodology** - Clear implementation plan builds confidence
- **Budget alignment** - Expenses must match stated activities
- **Collaborative approach** - Highlight partnerships and community support
- **Cultural competency** - Demonstrate understanding of populations served
- **Evaluation rigor** - Strong measurement plan signals accountability
- **Compelling writing** - Passionate but professional, active voice
